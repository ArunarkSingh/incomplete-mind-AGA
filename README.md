> *"The finish line does not exist. That, I argue, is precisely the point."*

## Overview

This repository contains the research behind **Asymptotic Goal Architecture (AGA)**, a theoretical framework proposing that genuine curiosity in artificial intelligence systems cannot be engineered directly — but emerges naturally as a structural consequence of pursuing a goal that is permanently and structurally incomplete.

Current AI systems are optimization engines. They move toward finish lines. And finish lines, by definition, end the race. This research asks a different question: what would give an AI system something it genuinely cannot stop thinking about?

## Core Idea

Existing approaches treat curiosity as a reward signal — analogous to training a dog with treats. The behavior appears, but the drive is absent. Remove the reward and the curiosity vanishes.

AGA proposes something fundamentally different. Give a system a goal that:

- Can always be approached but never fully reached
- Reveals more to pursue the closer you get
- Maintains a stable coherent identity over time

And curiosity does not need to be programmed. It emerges. Not as a feature. As a consequence.

Think of it as an infinite maze. Every door opened reveals new doors. Every skill developed reveals new skills required. Every answer generates deeper questions. The system keeps going not because it was rewarded for moving — but because the maze is what there is.

## The Four Conditions of an Asymptotic Goal

| Condition | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| **Approachability** | The system can make real, measurable progress |
| **Incompletability** | Full achievement is structurally impossible |
| **Generativity** | Progress expands rather than reduces what remains |
| **Coherence** | The goal maintains stable identity across time |

A goal satisfying all four conditions is an **asymptotic goal**. A system built around such a goal constitutes an **Asymptotic Goal Architecture**.

## The Curiosity Triad

Emergent curiosity requires three structural conditions:

1. **Permanent incompleteness** — the open question never closes
2. **Meaningful progress** — getting closer is always possible
3. **Generative revelation** — progress reveals more, not less

AGA satisfies all three by construction.

## Paper

**Title:** The Incomplete Mind: A Theoretical Framework for Emergent Curiosity in Artificial Intelligence through Asymptotic Goal Architecture

**Author:** Arunark Singh, University of British Columbia, Vancouver

**Status:** Submitted to arXiv

**Abstract:** Current artificial intelligence systems remain fundamentally reactive. They process inputs, match patterns, and generate outputs — but they do not wonder. This paper proposes Asymptotic Goal Architecture (AGA), a framework in which genuine curiosity emerges as the inevitable consequence of pursuing a goal that is permanently and structurally incomplete. This is a theoretical framework and research agenda inviting critical engagement from the research community.

- Chalmers (1995) — The hard problem of consciousness

## Research Agenda

**Stage 1:** Theoretical foundations and mathematical formalization of AGA conditions

**Stage 2:** Small-scale empirical validation on existing models

**Stage 3:** Scaled implementation and evaluation

**Stage 4:** The consciousness question — studying the relationship between architecture and experience
